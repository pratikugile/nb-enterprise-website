Tube Bending Die

The tube bending die is the foundational forming tool in the rotary draw bending process, determining the exact radius of the bend. Our dies are precision-machined from high-grade tool steel to ensure exact tube outer diameters and perfect wall support during the bending cycle. Used extensively in automotive exhaust manufacturing, furniture frames, and aerospace piping, these dies guarantee repeatable, wrinkle-free bends even on complex profiles.

  • Available in standard, tight, and large bend radii (1D to 3D+)
  • Manufactured from premium hardened alloy steel for extended wear resistance
  • Interchangeable grip sections for versatile application
  • Customizable profiles for round, square, or rectangular tubing

Mandrel

Mandrels are critical internal support tools used to prevent tube collapse, wrinkling, or flattening during tight-radius bending. Inserted into the tube before the bending operation, our mandrels flex with the bend to provide continuous internal support. We manufacture plug mandrels for simple bends and complex multi-ball articulating mandrels for extremely thin-walled tubing used in high-performance fluid transfer systems.

  • Configurations available: Plug, Form, Single-ball, and Multi-ball
  • Materials include Hardened Steel, Aluminum Bronze, and Chrome-plated options
  • High-flexibility links for smooth extraction post-bend
  • Precision ground to exact tube inner diameter tolerances

Clamping Die

The clamping die secures the straight section of the tube against the bend die, preventing any slippage during the drawing process. A perfectly matched clamping die is essential for maintaining the grip length without marring the tube's surface finish. Our clamp dies are engineered to provide maximum holding force while protecting the cosmetic appearance of materials like stainless steel, aluminum, and copper.

  • Available with smooth, serrated, or specialized grit-blasted grips
  • Length optimized based on material slip characteristics and bend difficulty
  • Precision matched tube groove to prevent surface distortion
  • Constructed from tough, shock-resistant steel alloys

Wiper Shoe

Positioned exactly at the tangent point of the bend, the wiper shoe (or wiper die) is crucial for preventing wrinkles on the inside radius of thin-walled tubes. By fitting perfectly into the groove of the bend die, it contains the material as it compresses. Our wiper shoes feature a precisely feathered edge that supports the tube seamlessly, drastically reducing scrap rates in critical aerospace and medical tube bending applications.

  • Manufactured from Aluminum Bronze (for steel) or Hardened Steel (for softer tubes)
  • Ultra-thin feathered edge machined to zero-clearance specifications
  • Available in square back or specialized mounting configurations
  • Designed to minimize galling and friction during the draw

Pressure Die

The pressure die follows the tube during the bending process, providing continuous lateral support to contain radial forces and prevent bursting or flattening. Essential for maintaining the tube's shape as it is drawn around the bend die, our pressure dies are crafted for extremely low friction. We offer both static and follower (roller) type pressure dies tailored to your specific CNC machine capabilities and material requirements.

  • Optimized length calculations based on the total arc of the bend
  • Available with integrated lubrication grooves for reduced friction
  • Precision ground tube groove for uniform pressure distribution
  • Heat-treated for maximum wear resistance in high-volume production

Collet

Collets (and collet pads) act as the primary gripping mechanism in the carriage chuck of a CNC tube bender, accurately pushing and rotating the tube between bends. A secure, non-damaging grip is vital for achieving precise distance-between-bends (DBB) and plane-of-bend (POB) tolerances. Our collets are custom-bored to fit specific tube profiles, ensuring maximum concentricity and holding power without crushing the material.

  • Custom bored to precise outer diameter tolerances
  • Available in solid steel or with replaceable insert pads
  • Surface finishes tailored to grip requirements (smooth to aggressively serrated)
  • Compatible with all major CNC tube bender carriage systems

Bend Die Insert

Bend die inserts are replaceable wear components located in the clamping section of the main bend die. Because the grip area experiences the highest stress and wear over time, using replaceable inserts extends the overall life of the expensive bend die body. Our inserts are precision-machined for a seamless fit, allowing for quick changeovers and consistent grip performance across long production runs.

  • Cost-effective alternative to replacing the entire bend die
  • Available with specialized grip patterns for different tube materials
  • Hardened to withstand extreme clamping forces without deformation
  • Engineered for quick, precise alignment during tool changes
